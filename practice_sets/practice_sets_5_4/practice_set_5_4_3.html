Full Rigor Spiral: use zeros, graphs, equations, and explanations to connect factored form to quadratic behavior. No workspace is included.
Unit 5.4 — Factored Form and Zeros
1.Unit 5.4 | DoK 1 | Zeros from equation

Identify the zeros of $y=(x+6)(x-1)$.

2.Unit 5.4 | DoK 2 | Write equation

Write a factored equation for a quadratic with zeros $-3$ and $5$ and leading coefficient $1$.

3.Unit 5.4 | DoK 3 | Multiple representations

Explain how the equation $y=(x+2)(x-4)$, the zeros, and the $x$-intercepts all communicate the same information.

4.Unit 5.4 | DoK 4 | Design and compare

Design two different quadratics with the same zeros but different shapes. Explain how their graphs would be similar and different.

Unit 5.3 Spiral — Special Factoring Patterns
5.Unit 5.3 | DoK 1 | Identify pattern

Classify $25x^2-49$ as a difference of squares, a perfect square trinomial, or neither.

6.Unit 5.3 | DoK 1 | Factor

Factor completely.

$$25x^2-49$$

7.Unit 5.3 | DoK 1 | Recognize not special

Explain why $x^2+8x+12$ is not a perfect square trinomial.

8.Unit 5.3 | DoK 1 | Complete square pattern

What number completes the perfect square trinomial $x^2+14x+\square$?

9.Unit 5.3 | DoK 2 | Factor PST

Factor completely.

$$4x^2+20x+25$$

10.Unit 5.3 | DoK 2 | Mixed practice

Factor each expression if possible using a special pattern.

  1. $x^2-121$
  2. $x^2+22x+121$
  3. $x^2+121$
11.Unit 5.3 | DoK 3 | Compare patterns

Compare $x^2-36$ and $x^2+12x+36$. How are the patterns different even though both include $36$?

12.Unit 5.3 | DoK 4 | Create and critique

Create a false special-pattern claim and then write a correction that would help a classmate avoid the misconception.
