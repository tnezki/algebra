Unit 5.5 — Comparing Quadratic Forms
1.Unit 5.5 | Form recognition

Name the form of each equation.

  1. $y=x^2-7x+10$
  2. $y=3(x+2)^2-8$
  3. $y=-2(x-5)(x+1)$
2.Unit 5.5 | Vertex form

What feature is easiest to identify from $y=-4(x-1)^2+20$? Identify the feature.

3.Unit 5.5 | Factored form

What feature is easiest to identify from $y=(x+6)(x-4)$? Identify the feature.

4.Unit 5.5 | Standard form

For $y=x^2-5x+6$, identify the y-intercept and explain how standard form helped.

5.Unit 5.5 | Graph comparison

Use the graph. Which model has the greater maximum or minimum value? Explain using the legend labels.

6.Unit 5.5 | Choose a form

A question asks, “When is the object on the ground?” Which quadratic form would be most efficient? Explain.

7.Unit 5.5 | Equivalence

Show that $y=(x-3)(x+2)$ can be written in standard form.

8.Unit 5.5 | Justification

Why might a student want more than one form of the same quadratic? Give two different features and the form that reveals each one.
