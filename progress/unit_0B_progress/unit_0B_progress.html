Unit 0B Practice by Depth of Knowledge

DOK 1 — Rungs 1–2Students can identify coordinates, graph vocabulary, inputs, outputs, basic patterns, and simple representation features accurately.

Format: 3–5 minute warm-up, quick check, vocabulary check, graph-reading check, or exit ticket

Purpose: Check whether students can recall vocabulary, identify coordinates, read basic tables and graphs, and recognize foundational features of patterns and relationships.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Coordinate Plane Fluency Check Can Be Assessed After Section 1 – Students identify axes, quadrants, ordered pairs, intercepts, and plotted points.
  2. Input-Output Vocabulary Check Can Be Assessed After Section 2 – Students identify inputs, outputs, rules, and missing values in simple tables.
  3. Linear Pattern Recognition Check Can Be Assessed After Section 3 – Students recognize constant change and basic linear pattern features.
  4. Representation Match Quick Check Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students match simple graphs, tables, equations, and descriptions.
What this tells the teacher:
Students who struggle here need more support with graph vocabulary, table reading, coordinate notation, and the basic language of relationships before they can reason about linear models.

DOK 2 — Rungs 3–4Students can apply input-output rules, find constant change, write simple linear relationships, and connect tables, graphs, equations, and contexts.

Format: Exit ticket, partner check, whiteboard response, short written task, mini-quiz, or graph-table task

Purpose: Check whether students can apply input-output rules, determine constant rates of change, write linear relationships, and connect graphs, tables, equations, and contexts.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Input-Output Rule Task Can Be Assessed After Section 2 – Students complete a table, use a rule, and explain what the input and output represent.
  2. Rate of Change Task Can Be Assessed After Section 3 – Students determine constant change from a table, graph, or visual pattern.
  3. Writing a Linear Rule Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 – Students write an equation from a table, graph, context, or growing pattern.
  4. Comparing Representations Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students determine whether two representations describe the same relationship and support the decision.

DOK 3 — Rungs 5–6Students can justify linearity, explain rate of change, analyze representation structure, and critique errors using mathematical evidence.

Format: Constructed response, representation analysis, strategy comparison, discourse prompt, or error analysis

Purpose: Check whether students can justify linearity, explain rate of change, analyze representation structure, and defend conclusions using mathematical evidence.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Linearity Justification Task Can Be Assessed After Section 3 – Students decide whether a relationship is linear and justify the conclusion from a table, graph, or context.
  2. Representation Analysis Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students analyze a graph, table, equation, and context to explain which features match.
  3. Strategy Comparison Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 – Students compare two ways to write a linear relationship and explain which is clearer or more efficient.
  4. Misleading Representation Critique Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students critique a flawed graph, table, or rule and correct the reasoning.
What this tells the teacher:
This level reveals whether students understand relationships as connected representations rather than isolated procedures. These tasks identify who can explain, justify, critique, and transfer linear readiness concepts.

DOK 4 — Rungs 7–8Students can model unfamiliar relationships with multiple representations and justify conclusions or recommendations using mathematical evidence.

Format: Brief performance task, modeling task, design task, critique-and-revise task, or decision-making task

Purpose: Check whether students can transfer graph, table, pattern, rule, and linear relationship understanding to unfamiliar situations and justify decisions.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Design a Linear Pattern Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 – Students create a pattern, table, graph, and rule that all represent the same relationship.
  2. Compare Two Plans Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students model and compare two linear situations and justify a recommendation.
  3. Critique and Redesign Representation Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students evaluate a flawed representation and revise it so the table, graph, rule, and context agree.
  4. Multi-Representation Modeling Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students create and analyze multiple representations for an unfamiliar relationship and communicate conclusions using evidence.
What this tells the teacher:
DOK 4 tasks reveal whether students can use multiple representations to make decisions, design models, critique reasoning, and communicate conclusions. These should remain short performance tasks rather than large projects.
