Unit 0A Practice by Depth of Knowledge

DOK 1 — Rungs 1–2Students can identify and use rational numbers, basic algebraic notation, and foundational vocabulary accurately.

Format: 3–5 minute warm-up, quick check, proficiency check, or exit ticket

Purpose: Check whether students can recall vocabulary, perform rational number operations, use algebraic notation correctly, and demonstrate foundational numerical fluency.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Rational Number Fluency Check Can Be Assessed After Section 1 – Students perform operations with integers, fractions, and decimals.
  2. Number Sense Retrieval Task Can Be Assessed After Section 1 – Students estimate, compare, and evaluate numerical expressions.
  3. Order of Operations Quick Check Can Be Assessed After Section 2 – Students evaluate expressions involving multiple operations and exponents.
  4. Vocabulary and Notation Check Can Be Assessed After Section 3 – Students identify variables, coefficients, terms, expressions, equations, and inequalities.
What this tells the teacher:
Students who struggle here need additional fluency support before deeper algebraic reasoning can occur. Difficulties often reveal gaps in prerequisite arithmetic or mathematical vocabulary.

DOK 2 — Rungs 3–4Students can evaluate expressions, apply order of operations, simplify expressions, and carry out routine equations or inequalities.

Format: Exit ticket, whiteboard response, partner check, short written task, or mini-quiz

Purpose: Check whether students can evaluate expressions, simplify algebraic expressions, apply properties, and solve routine equations and inequalities.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Expression Evaluation Task Can Be Assessed After Section 2 – Students evaluate expressions using substitution and order of operations.
  2. Equivalent Expression Task Can Be Assessed After Section 3 – Students simplify expressions and justify why expressions are equivalent.
  3. Equation Solving Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 – Students solve one-step and multi-step equations and verify solutions.
  4. Inequality Graphing Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students solve inequalities and represent solution sets on number lines.
What this tells the teacher:
Students at this level can generally carry out procedures and use appropriate algebraic tools. These assessments reveal whether students understand how procedures connect to algebraic structure and representation.

DOK 3 — Rungs 5–6Students can explain reasoning, justify solution methods, analyze structure, verify solutions, and critique errors.

Format: Constructed response, discourse prompt, strategy comparison, error analysis, or written explanation

Purpose: Check whether students can explain reasoning, justify procedures, analyze mathematical structure, and critique mathematical arguments.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Strategy Comparison Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 – Students solve an equation using two approaches and compare efficiency and clarity.
  2. Error Analysis Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students critique incorrect work involving equations or inequalities and explain the misconception.
  3. Structure and Properties Task Can Be Assessed After Section 3 – Students explain how properties of operations justify simplification steps.
  4. Solution Justification Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students defend whether a proposed solution is valid and support their reasoning with mathematical evidence.
What this tells the teacher:
This level reveals whether students truly understand why procedures work rather than simply following steps. These tasks identify misconceptions, reasoning gaps, and readiness for future modeling work.

DOK 4 — Rungs 7–8Students can model unfamiliar situations with expressions, equations, or inequalities and justify conclusions using mathematical evidence.

Format: Performance task, modeling task, critique-and-revise task, design task, or extended application

Purpose: Check whether students can transfer foundational algebra skills to unfamiliar situations, model real-world contexts, and justify conclusions.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Budget Constraint Modeling Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students create and interpret inequalities to model spending constraints and justify recommendations.
  2. Design an Equation Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 – Students create a real-world situation represented by a given equation and explain the meaning of the solution.
  3. Critique and Revise Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students evaluate flawed mathematical reasoning, identify errors, and rewrite a valid solution process.
  4. Multi-Representation Modeling Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 – Students analyze a situation using numerical reasoning, expressions, equations, and inequalities, then communicate conclusions using mathematical evidence.
What this tells the teacher:
DOK 4 tasks reveal whether students can transfer foundational algebra skills beyond routine exercises. These assessments emphasize reasoning, communication, modeling, and decision making rather than procedural execution alone.
