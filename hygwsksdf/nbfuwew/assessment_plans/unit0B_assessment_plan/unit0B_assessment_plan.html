Algebra 1 - Unit 0B: Functions and Linear Readiness

Essential Standard

Students will analyze patterns, relationships, and linear representations in order to prepare for function analysis, modeling, and systems of equations.
Section 0B.1: Coordinate Plane and Graph Interpretation

Learning Target: Students will interpret and create graphs on the coordinate plane while connecting points, axes, intercepts, and graphical information to mathematical situations.

I Can Statements

  • I can plot points on a coordinate plane.
  • I can identify coordinates from a graph.
  • I can interpret information from graphs.
  • I can connect graphs and tables.
  • I can describe what points and intercepts mean in context.

Key Representations

  • Coordinate planes
  • Ordered pairs
  • Graphs
  • Tables
  • Contextual situations

Modeling Opportunities

  • distance versus time graphs
  • map and location contexts
  • data displayed on coordinate grids
Section 0B.2: Patterns and Input-Output Rules

Learning Target: Students will identify patterns and represent relationships using input-output rules, tables, words, and symbols.

I Can Statements

  • I can identify patterns in tables.
  • I can determine outputs from rules.
  • I can determine rules from patterns.
  • I can describe relationships using words and symbols.
  • I can explain what an input and output represent.

Key Representations

  • Tables
  • Input-output rules
  • Equations
  • Verbal descriptions
  • Contexts

Modeling Opportunities

  • pricing rules
  • points earned over time
  • tiles or figures growing by a rule
Section 0B.3: Linear Patterns and Rate of Change

Learning Target: Students will recognize constant rate of change in tables, graphs, visual patterns, and situations.

I Can Statements

  • I can identify constant rate of change.
  • I can compare rates of change.
  • I can recognize linear relationships.
  • I can explain how quantities change.
  • I can use a pattern to make predictions.

Key Representations

  • Tables
  • Graphs
  • Equations
  • Tile patterns
  • Contexts

Modeling Opportunities

  • growing tile patterns
  • walking distance
  • earnings over time
  • savings plans
Section 0B.4: Writing Linear Relationships

Learning Target: Students will represent linear relationships using tables, graphs, equations, and contextual descriptions.

I Can Statements

  • I can create tables for linear relationships.
  • I can graph linear relationships.
  • I can write equations from tables and graphs.
  • I can interpret equations in context.
  • I can identify starting values and constant change.

Key Representations

  • Graphs
  • Tables
  • Equations
  • Contexts
  • Visual patterns

Modeling Opportunities

  • subscription costs
  • ticket or admission costs
  • linear tile patterns
  • constant-rate savings
Section 0B.5: Comparing Representations

Learning Target: Students will compare relationships using equations, graphs, tables, visual patterns, and contexts, then justify conclusions using mathematical evidence.

I Can Statements

  • I can determine whether representations match.
  • I can identify important features from representations.
  • I can justify conclusions using mathematical evidence.
  • I can decide which representation is most useful for a question.

Assessment Methods by DOK Level

DOK 1 - Rungs 1-2

Format: 3-5 minute warm-up, quick check, vocabulary check, graph-reading check, or exit ticket

Purpose: Check whether students can recall vocabulary, identify coordinates, read basic tables and graphs, and recognize foundational features of patterns and relationships.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Coordinate Plane Fluency Check Can Be Assessed After Section 1 - Students identify axes, quadrants, ordered pairs, intercepts, and plotted points.
  2. Input-Output Vocabulary Check Can Be Assessed After Section 2 - Students identify inputs, outputs, rules, and missing values in simple tables.
  3. Linear Pattern Recognition Check Can Be Assessed After Section 3 - Students recognize constant change and basic linear pattern features.
  4. Representation Match Quick Check Can Be Assessed After Section 5 - Students match simple graphs, tables, equations, and descriptions.
What this tells the teacher:
Students who struggle here need more support with graph vocabulary, table reading, coordinate notation, and the basic language of relationships before they can reason about linear models.
DOK 2 - Rungs 3-4

Format: Exit ticket, partner check, whiteboard response, short written task, mini-quiz, or graph-table task

Purpose: Check whether students can apply input-output rules, determine constant rates of change, write linear relationships, and connect graphs, tables, equations, and contexts.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Input-Output Rule Task Can Be Assessed After Section 2 - Students complete a table, use a rule, and explain what the input and output represent.
  2. Rate of Change Task Can Be Assessed After Section 3 - Students determine constant change from a table, graph, or visual pattern.
  3. Writing a Linear Rule Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 - Students write an equation from a table, graph, context, or growing pattern.
  4. Comparing Representations Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 - Students determine whether two representations describe the same relationship and support the decision.
What this tells the teacher:
Students at this level can generally use procedures and representations, but the assessment reveals whether they understand how starting value, constant change, and representation features connect.
DOK 3 - Rungs 5-6

Format: Constructed response, representation analysis, strategy comparison, discourse prompt, or error analysis

Purpose: Check whether students can justify linearity, explain rate of change, analyze representation structure, and defend conclusions using mathematical evidence.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Linearity Justification Task Can Be Assessed After Section 3 - Students decide whether a relationship is linear and justify the conclusion from a table, graph, or context.
  2. Representation Analysis Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 - Students analyze a graph, table, equation, and context to explain which features match.
  3. Strategy Comparison Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 - Students compare two ways to write a linear relationship and explain which is clearer or more efficient.
  4. Misleading Representation Critique Can Be Assessed After Section 5 - Students critique a flawed graph, table, or rule and correct the reasoning.
What this tells the teacher:
This level reveals whether students understand relationships as connected representations rather than isolated procedures. These tasks identify who can explain, justify, critique, and transfer linear readiness concepts.
DOK 4 - Rungs 7-8

Format: Brief performance task, modeling task, design task, critique-and-revise task, or decision-making task

Purpose: Check whether students can transfer graph, table, pattern, rule, and linear relationship understanding to unfamiliar situations and justify decisions.

Assessment Ideas

  1. Design a Linear Pattern Task Can Be Assessed After Section 4 - Students create a pattern, table, graph, and rule that all represent the same relationship.
  2. Compare Two Plans Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 - Students model and compare two linear situations and justify a recommendation.
  3. Critique and Redesign Representation Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 - Students evaluate a flawed representation and revise it so the table, graph, rule, and context agree.
  4. Multi-Representation Modeling Task Can Be Assessed After Section 5 - Students create and analyze multiple representations for an unfamiliar relationship and communicate conclusions using evidence.
What this tells the teacher:
DOK 4 tasks reveal whether students can use multiple representations to make decisions, design models, critique reasoning, and communicate conclusions. These should remain short performance tasks rather than large projects.

Summative Assessment Plan

Unit 0B Summative Assessment Unit 0B Assessed During Unit 2

Assessment is designed for a 45-minute paper-only time limit and is built around balanced evidence rather than a fixed question-count breakdown. Each version includes a mix of graph interpretation, table and rule work, linear pattern analysis, representation tasks, and short reasoning prompts aligned to the unit learning targets and Depth of Knowledge expectations.

Assessments emphasize coordinate-plane fluency, input-output reasoning, constant rate of change, writing linear relationships, comparing representations, interpreting contexts, mathematical communication, and justification of reasoning.

Summative assessments are intentionally focused on DoK 1-3 evidence within a paper-only environment. Extended DoK 4 modeling, critique, design, and performance tasks are assessed separately through formative assessments, collaborative activities, investigations, discourse tasks, and performance assessments rather than embedded directly into the summative assessment.

Because of the spiraling course structure, Unit 0B is assessed during Unit 2 after students have had additional opportunities to revisit and strengthen understanding through retrieval practice, warm-ups, formative assessments, and subsequent instruction.

Holistic Rubric

A - Highly Proficient

The student demonstrates a strong and flexible understanding of Unit 0B concepts: coordinate graphs, input-output rules, patterns, linear relationships, and comparing representations. Work is accurate, well-organized, and supported by clear reasoning. The student consistently connects graphs, tables, equations, and contexts while justifying conclusions using precise mathematical language.

B - Proficient

The student demonstrates solid understanding of the essential Unit 0B concepts. Most work is accurate, and the student can complete standard and moderately complex tasks independently. Explanations are generally clear, and the student demonstrates appropriate use of notation, representations, and linear relationship reasoning.

C - Developing Proficiency

The student demonstrates partial but passable understanding of Unit 0B concepts. The student can complete many routine tasks but may need support with graph interpretation, notation, rate of change, writing rules, comparing representations, or mathematical communication. Work may contain errors or incomplete explanations, but there is sufficient evidence that the student understands the major concepts.

Not Yet - Insufficient Evidence

The student does not yet demonstrate sufficient understanding of the essential Unit 0B concepts. Work shows major errors, missing reasoning, weak graph or table fluency, misunderstanding of input-output relationships, or difficulty connecting graphs, tables, equations, and contexts. Additional instruction, practice, and reassessment may be needed before proficiency can be demonstrated.
